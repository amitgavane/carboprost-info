Carboprost
Prostaglandin F2α analogue · Uterotonic agent
Class: Prostaglandin analogue Sub-class: PGF2α analogue Route: Deep IM injection Used in: PPH · 2nd-trimester termination

Dose & Route

Standard Regimen
Single dose
250 micrograms (0.25 mg)
Route
Deep intramuscular (IM) injection
Repeat
Every 15–90 minutes
Maximum
Up to 8 doses (total max 2 mg)

*Dosing must always be individualized and used under specialist supervision in a hospital setting.

Indications

Obstetric Uses
  • Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) due to uterine atony (after failure of oxytocin and ergometrine)
  • Second-trimester medical abortion (≥13 weeks) – for induction or expulsion
  • Evacuation of retained products of conception (RPOC) in mid-trimester
  • Termination in missed / incomplete abortion (mid-trimester)

Mechanism of Action

PGF2α Receptor Agonist

Carboprost is a synthetic analogue of prostaglandin F2α that binds to prostaglandin F (PGF) receptors in smooth muscle, particularly in the uterus.

  • Stimulates strong uterine contractions and increases uterine tone.
  • Causes vasoconstriction of uterine blood vessels, helping to reduce bleeding and control PPH.
  • Acts on bronchial smooth muscle → may cause bronchoconstriction.
  • Acts on gastrointestinal smooth muscle → can lead to vomiting and diarrhea.
↑ Uterine contractility ↑ Uterine tone ↓ Uterine blood loss Bronchoconstriction GI smooth muscle stimulation

Primary therapeutic effect: uterotonic action for control of PPH and facilitation of mid-trimester termination.

Important Drug Interactions

Use with caution
  • Other uterotonics (oxytocin, methylergometrine): ↑ risk of hypertonic uterus and uterine rupture.
  • Antihypertensives: Blood pressure may fluctuate; prostaglandins may reduce antihypertensive effect.
  • Bronchodilators (β2-agonists): Opposing actions – bronchodilators relax bronchial smooth muscle, while carboprost causes bronchoconstriction.
  • Magnesium sulfate: Slight decrease in uterotonic effect.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Monitor closely
Common
  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Fever, chills
  • Flushing
Serious / Important
  • Bronchospasm, bronchoconstriction (contraindicated in asthma)
  • Hypertension
  • Tachycardia
  • Pulmonary edema
  • Uterine hyperstimulation
  • Anaphylactoid reaction
⚠️
Key safety note: Carboprost should be used only in a monitored setting with resuscitation facilities available. Avoid use in patients with asthma or significant cardiopulmonary disease.
